We propose a novel architectural concept, accelerator polymorphism, whereby a single physical substrate dynamically shape-shifts from one specialized execution model to another without sacrificing the efficiency of specialization. We instantiate this concept in Morphatron, a microarchitecture that dynamically shape-shifts across five execution morphas—queue-centric SIMD, graph, tree, vector, and systolic—each tailored to distinct algorithmic domains in robotics.
